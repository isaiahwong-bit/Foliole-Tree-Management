Field Notes

Victoria's iconic trees

Four of the trees Jordan works with across Melbourne and greater Victoria — what makes each remarkable, and what they need to thrive.

1
Eucalyptus regnans

Mountain Ash

The tallest flowering plant on Earth. In Victoria's Central Highlands the best specimens push past 90 metres — among the tallest trees anywhere.

Jordan's note: Towering heights mean only experienced climbers should work them — and they regenerate from fire, so timing matters.

2
Eucalyptus camaldulensis

River Red Gum

Lines the Murray and Victoria's waterways and can live 500–1,000 years. Australia's most widespread eucalypt.

Jordan's note: Famous for dropping heavy limbs without warning — regular health and risk assessment is essential near homes.

3
Eucalyptus pauciflora

Snow Gum

The sculptural survivor of the Victorian Alps — twisted, multi-coloured trunks that shrug off snow and temperatures below -10°C.

Jordan's note: Slow-growing and long-lived; prized as ornamentals, they reward careful formative pruning.

4
Eucalyptus viminalis

Manna Gum

A favourite koala food tree that sheds bark in long ribbons and weeps a sweet, edible "manna" — the source of its name.

Jordan's note: Shedding ribbons and habitat value shape how and when it should be pruned — wildlife first.
